# Candidate Rhetoric Techniques (Future Reference)

These rhetoric techniques were identified as potentially missing from the current `RHETORIC_TECHNIQUES` data but deemed too commonplace for immediate inclusion. They may be added in a future release if markup granularity warrants it.

## Pathos

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Repetition / Anaphora** | Repeating words or phrases at the start of successive clauses for emphasis | "I have a dream… I have a dream… I have a dream…" |
| **Hyperbole** | Deliberate exaggeration to emphasize a point | "I've told you a million times." |
| **Irony / Sarcasm** | Saying the opposite of what is meant for rhetorical effect | "Oh sure, because that worked so well last time." |
| **Euphemism / Dysphemism** | Softening (or hardening) language to shape perception | "Passed away" vs. "kicked the bucket" |

## Logos

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Rhetorical Question** | Asking a question to make a point rather than to get an answer | "Isn't it time we did something about this?" |
| **Antithesis** | Juxtaposing contrasting ideas in balanced phrases | "Ask not what your country can do for you — ask what you can do for your country." |
| **Rule of Three (Tricolon)** | Presenting ideas in groups of three for rhythm and memorability | "Government of the people, by the people, for the people." |
| **Parallelism** | Using similar grammatical structures for rhythm and emphasis | "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds…" |
| **Reframing** | Presenting an issue from a different perspective to shift interpretation | Calling a tax increase an "investment in our future." |

## Ethos

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Concession (Concessio)** | Acknowledging the opponent's point before countering it | "I agree the cost is high, but the benefits far outweigh it." |
| **Allusion** | Referencing well-known texts, events, or figures to borrow their authority | "Like Churchill once said…" |
